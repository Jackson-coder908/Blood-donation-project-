Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know about donating blood with LifeFlow.

Before Donating

Whole blood donations can be made every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donations can be made every 7 days, up to 24 times per year. Plasma donations can be made every 28 days. Double red cell donations require a 112-day wait.

Eat a healthy, iron-rich meal at least 2 hours before donating. Foods like red meat, spinach, beans, and fortified cereals help boost your iron levels. Avoid fatty foods like french fries or hamburgers before donation, as they can affect testing of the donated blood.

No — in fact, please do NOT fast before donating. Eating a nutritious meal and drinking plenty of water beforehand helps prevent dizziness and ensures a better experience. Staying hydrated is especially important.

Most common medications are fine. However, some medications like blood thinners (e.g., warfarin), certain acne medications (isotretinoin), and some antibiotics may require a waiting period. Please inform staff of all medications at your screening — they'll advise you accordingly.

During Donation

Most donors feel only a brief pinch when the needle is inserted. The actual donation process is comfortable and painless. Our trained staff do everything possible to make you at ease. Many donors read, use their phones, or chat with staff during the process.

The actual blood draw takes just 8–10 minutes for a whole blood donation. Including registration, the health screening, and the recovery/refreshment period, plan for about 45–60 minutes for your first visit and around 30–40 minutes for subsequent visits.

Absolutely. All needles and collection bags are sterile, used once, and then safely discarded. There is zero risk of contracting any disease from donating blood. Our centers follow strict FDA and AABB standards.

After Donating

Most donors feel completely fine and return to normal activities. Some people experience mild lightheadedness or fatigue immediately after. We encourage you to rest for 15 minutes and enjoy refreshments before leaving. Avoid strenuous exercise for the rest of the day.

Your body replaces plasma within 24 hours. Red blood cells are fully replenished within 4–6 weeks, which is why there's a 56-day minimum between whole blood donations. Drinking extra fluids and eating iron-rich foods speeds up recovery.

Yes! First-time donors receive a donor card with their blood type. We also test every donation for infectious diseases and will notify you if anything requires medical attention — this is one of the hidden health benefits of regular donation.

Blood Types & Impact

O-negative is the universal donor type — it can be given to anyone in an emergency, making it critically important. O-positive is also always in high demand as the most common type. AB plasma is universal for plasma donations. All blood types are needed — your donation matters regardless of type.

Your blood is tested, processed, and separated into components (red cells, plasma, platelets). It's then distributed to local hospitals and trauma centers. Most blood is used within 42 days of donation, which is why a continuous supply of donors is so critical.
